Police Killings in the United States in 2015

Introduction

In the country that is famous for personal liberty and individualism, a virus is spreading. The virus does not have a concrete shape or form, and it infects people inconspicuously by creating fears and letting the blood spill with cold eyes. Some people call it the dedicated agent of the New Jim Crow, and some, who refuse to stay silent, have relentlessly acted against it with courage. The virus has a common name of police killing.

In year 2015 alone, more than 1500 people lost their lives on the hands of police officers. The Washington Post collected information about these 1500 people and incidents that led to their death and conducted a series of data journalism works. For research purpose, I have scraped the data from the website, which I have replicated some of the graphs with the goal of rethinking what happened in that year and what implications we can get from the loss.

African Americans are overrepresented in police killing victims

Narrative: In year 2015, 378 out of 1537 victims murdered by police officers in the US were Blacks. This group, however, only represented 13ish % of the nation's population. Comparatively, half of the victims were Whites, the racial group that accounted for over 70% of the population. Police officers disproportionately killed racial minorities (with the exception of Asian), but why?
